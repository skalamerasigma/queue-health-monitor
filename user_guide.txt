Status Thresholds & On-Track Targets
The application uses standardized thresholds to determine TSE queue health status. Understanding these thresholds is essential for interpreting all metrics throughout the application.

üéØ
On-Track Target: 80%+
Team Goal: The target is for 80% or more of TSEs to be "on track" at any given time. This ensures optimal queue health and customer experience.

Why 80%: This target accounts for normal variations in workload, temporary spikes, and allows for some TSEs to be temporarily over limits while maintaining overall team performance.

‚úÖ
On-Track Calculation
Definition: A TSE is considered "on track" if they meet BOTH of the following thresholds simultaneously:

Open Conversations: ‚â§ 5 active, non-snoozed conversations
Waiting on TSE: ‚â§ 5 conversations snoozed with the "snooze.waiting-on-tse" tag
Important Notes:

Both conditions must be met - if either threshold is exceeded, the TSE is not on track
"Waiting on TSE" specifically refers to conversations with the "snooze.waiting-on-tse" tag, not all snoozed conversations
Conversations waiting on customer (with "snooze.waiting-on-customer" tags) do not count toward the waiting-on-TSE threshold
Calculation Formula: For any group of TSEs, On-Track % = (Number of TSEs meeting both thresholds / Total TSEs) √ó 100

Status Levels
Four distinct status levels:

‚≠êÔ∏è
Outstanding
0 open AND 0 waiting on TSE

Perfect performance - no active workload. TSE has completed all assigned work and has no pending items.

On Track
‚â§5 open AND ‚â§5 waiting

Within acceptable limits. TSE is managing their queue effectively and meeting performance standards.

Over Limit - Needs Attention
>5 open OR >5 waiting

Exceeds thresholds and requires attention. May need workload redistribution or support.

üö®
Alert Thresholds vs. Soft Limits
Two-Tier System: The application uses two sets of thresholds for different purposes:

Soft Limits (On-Track Thresholds):
Open Chats: ‚â§ 5
Waiting on TSE: ‚â§ 5
Used to determine "On Track" status. TSEs meeting these limits are considered compliant.

Alert Thresholds:
Open Chats Alert: 6+ conversations
Waiting on TSE Alert: 7+ conversations
Trigger notifications when exceeded. These are higher thresholds that indicate a TSE needs immediate attention.

Why Different: Soft limits define acceptable performance, while alert thresholds identify situations requiring immediate intervention. This two-tier system prevents alert fatigue while maintaining clear performance standards.

Alert Types & Severity
Two Alert Types:

Open Chat Alerts: Triggered when a TSE has 6 or more open conversations
Waiting On TSE Alerts: Triggered when a TSE has 7 or more conversations waiting on them (tagged "snooze.waiting-on-tse")
Severity Levels: Alerts are automatically assigned severity based on how far over the threshold:

High Severity (üî¥): TSE exceeds threshold by 3+ conversations
Open Chats: 9+ conversations

Medium Severity (üü°): TSE exceeds threshold but by less than 3 conversations
Open Chats: 6 to 8 conversations
Waiting on TSE: 7 to 9 conversations
Real-Time Updates: Alerts update automatically as conversation counts change, ensuring you always have current information.
